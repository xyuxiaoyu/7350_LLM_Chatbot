What is an LLM large language model?

Research on LLMS in both academia and industry has made significant progress to a large extent. One notable advancement is the launch of ChatGPT, which has attracted widespread attention from society. The technological development of LLM has had a significant impact on the entire AI community, which will completely transform the way we develop and use AI algorithms.

The definition of large language models

Large Language Model (LLM) is an artificial intelligence model, aiming to understand and generate human language. They are trained on a large amount of text data and can perform a wide range of tasks, including text summary, translation, sentiment analysis, and so on. LLMS are characterized by their large scale, containing billions of parameters, which help them learn complex patterns in language data. These models are usually based on deep learning architectures, such as converters, which helps them achieve impressive performance on various NLP tasks.

The emerging capabilities of large language models

Context learning

GPT-3 officially introduces the context learning ability: Assuming that the language model has already provided natural language instructions and multiple task descriptions.

It can generate the expected output of the test instance by completing the word sequence of the input text without the need for additional training or gradient updates.

Instructions are followed

By fine-tuning the mixture of multi-task datasets formatted with natural language descriptions, LLMS perform well on tiny tasks that are also described in the form of instructions. Under this capability, instruction tuning enables LLMS to execute new tasks by understanding task instructions without using explicit templates, which can significantly enhance the generalization ability.

Step-by-step reasoning

For small language models, it is usually difficult to solve complex tasks involving multiple reasoning steps, such as word problems in mathematics. Meanwhile, through the chain of thought reasoning strategy, LLMS can solve such tasks and obtain the final answer by utilizing the prompt mechanism involving intermediate reasoning steps. This ability might be acquired through code training.

From 2017 to the present

The History of Language Models

In 2017, Google launched the transformer model. In 2018, Google proposed the Bert model. Then came GPT 2, with parameters ranging from 340 megabytes to 1 billion, 1.5 billion, then 8.3 billion, then 17 billion, and finally 175 billion for GPT-3.

The earliest one came out in 2017, which is the GPT we know. In the name of GPT, there is a character called transformer, which is the transformer model. It emerged in 2017, which is actually quite early. Therefore, in the field of computers, 2017 can be attributed to the products of the previous era. Then in 2018, the first generation of GPT came out. At that time, it was not good. Relatively speaking, it was rather poor and its performance was not good either. It was like a toy. Then in 2018, Google launched a new model called BERT, but all these models were developed based on the transformer model that Google had previously introduced. Then in 2019, open AI had nothing special except for GPT 2. It was unable to generate a noun with smooth and fluent language logic. You could tell at a glance that it was written by a machine.

However, in May 2020, after the release of GPT 3, there were actually very significant changes. The performance of GPT 3 was much better than that of GPT 2, and the order of magnitude of its number parameters was approximately 2 to 10 times or more than that of GPT.

The training methods of large language models

Training a language model requires feeding it a large amount of textual data, which the model uses to learn the structure, syntax, and semantics of human language.This process is usually done by unsupervised learning, self-monitoring learning technology. In self-supervised learning, the model generates its own label for the input data by predicting the next word or label in the sequence and gives the previous word.

  • Pre-training

During the pre-training stage, the model learns from a huge and diverse dataset, typically containing billions of words from various sources, such as websites, books, and articles. This stage allows the model to learn general language patterns and representations.

  • Fine-tuning

During the fine-tuning stage, the model is further trained on a more specific and smaller dataset related to the target task or domain. Such training helps the model fine-tune its understanding and enables it to adapt to the special requirements of the task.

The key technologies of LLM

Scaling

More model parameters, data volume and training calculations can effectively improve the model performance.

Training

Distributed training strategies and some optimization tricks to enhance training stability and effectiveness. In addition, GPT-4 also proposed to establish some special engineering facilities to predict the performance of large models through the performance of small models (predictable scaling).

Ability eliciting

Designing appropriate task instructions or specific context learning strategies can stimulate the ability of LLMS to learn on large-scale predictions.

Alignment tuning

To avoid the model outputting some responses that are unsafe or do not conform to human positive values, InstructGPT uses the RLHF (reinforcement learning with human feedback) technology to achieve this goal.

Tools manipulation

To make up for the problems that the model is not good at non-text output tasks and lacks real-time information, enable the model to use tools such as calculators, search engines or install plugins for the model.
